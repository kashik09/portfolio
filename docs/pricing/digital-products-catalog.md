## PRICING STRATEGY SUMMARY

### Pricing Tiers Explained

**Why Three Tiers?**
- **Personal ($49-$149):** Students, hobbyists, side projects
  - Lower price = higher volume
  - Word-of-mouth marketing
  - Upsell to Commercial later

- **Commercial ($99-$599):** Freelancers, small businesses, startups
  - Sweet spot (most sales)
  - Covers business use
  - Justified by time savings

- **Team/Enterprise ($249-$2,499):** Agencies, dev teams, companies
  - Higher price = more support
  - Unlimited developers
  - Custom features/support

### Price Anchoring

```
Personal: $149
Commercial: $299 (2x, appears reasonable)
Team: $599 (4x, makes Commercial look like a deal)
```

Most customers choose Commercial tier (~60-70% of sales).

### Time-Based Pricing Formula

```
Estimated Development Time Ã— Hourly Rate Ã— Discount Factor = Base Price

Example: Next.js SaaS Starter
60 hours Ã— $50/hr Ã— 0.05 = $150 (Personal)
60 hours Ã— $50/hr Ã— 0.10 = $300 (Commercial)
60 hours Ã— $50/hr Ã— 0.20 = $600 (Team)

Discount Factor:
- 5% (Personal) - selling in volume
- 10% (Commercial) - target market
- 20% (Team) - premium tier
```
